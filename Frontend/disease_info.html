Knowledge Base

Disease Intelligence Library

Browse known structural vulnerabilities, identify visual symptoms, and deploy standardized treatments to protect your yield.

Wheat Leaf Rust

Small, circular, orange-red pustules on the upper surface of leaves. Rapidly reduces photosynthetic area.

Standard Tx: Triazole fungicides (Tebuconazole)
Organic Tx: Destroy volunteer wheat, plant resistant cultivars

Stripe Rust (Yellow)

Yellowish-orange pustules arranged in highly visible stripes parallel to leaf veins.

Standard Tx: Apply Azoxystrobin or Propiconazole early
Organic Tx: Adjust planting density to improve airflow

Stem Rust (Black)

Brick-red to black elongated pustules primarily located on structural stems and leaf sheaths.

Standard Tx: Immediate systemic fungicide application
Organic Tx: Eradicate barberry bushes (alternate host)

Powdery Mildew

White to pale gray, fuzzy or powdery colonies coating the upper surfaces of leaves and lower stems.

Standard Tx: Sulfur-based or modern systemic fungicides
Organic Tx: Avoid excessive nitrogen fertility, apply Potassium Bicarbonate

Septoria Tritici Blotch

Irregularly shaped, clustered brown to tan lesions housing small dark fungal fruiting bodies inside.

Standard Tx: SDHI + Triazole premixes during flag leaf emergence
Organic Tx: Crop rotation and deep plowing to bury residues

Fusarium Head Blight

Premature bleaching of spikelets producing pink/orange mold on the head, severely destroying grain quality.

Standard Tx: Prothioconazole applied strictly at anthesis (flowering)
Organic Tx: Avoid planting after corn to naturally break disease cycle

Tan Spot

Tan, oval spots bordered by noticeable yellow halos taking shape primarily on lower leaves.

Standard Tx: Early application of strobilurin/triazole mixtures
Organic Tx: Implement strict stubble management to dry environment

Loose Smut

Wheat heads are entirely replaced by a mass of dark, olive-black, dusty fungal spores.

Standard Tx: Fungicidal seed treatments (Carboxin) prior to planting
Organic Tx: Hot water seed treatment (54°C) or certified clean seed
